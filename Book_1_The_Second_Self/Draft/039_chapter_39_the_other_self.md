The extended block had been authorized before she decided she wanted it.

Month Three, week fourteen. CIB-4, upper tier—the **Second interface** suite where identity was still bureaucratic enough to schedule and dangerous enough to schedule anyway. The calendar line from yesterday had read **extended session · authorized · comparison slot available**; the intake form this morning read **extended immersion · investigator overload · post-stutter policy window**. Same room. Different permission. Longer leash.

Mara badged in with coffee she hadn't finished and unease she hadn't filed—the private kind that lived beside open sidebar flags and mentor margin notes and did not become case tabs because some wrong feelings arrived without coordinates. The suite smelled like filtered air and glass cleaner. No Veil bloom. No overlay hooks by design. Policy still called side-by-side review **clinical**, which meant honest in the way bureau rooms meant honest: fluorescent, recorded, not kind.

The block was not side-by-side only.

The dock at home accepted the extended handshake with the polite chime of a body asked to wait while attention traveled elsewhere. Mara settled into the bureau chair in the suite—flesh in the room because some procedures still required it—while her primary channel dimmed toward dock posture: shoulders finding the familiar slump, breath slowing, the body learning again what it had learned in Month Two and pretended was temporary. **Extended** meant hours, not modules. Immersion, not comparison clips.

At home her primary hands rested on armrests she wasn't watching. Weight in the thighs. Dry mouth. The ordinary tax of a body kept available for return. The suite monitors showed dock vitals in a footer Mara usually ignored—heart rate steady, attention map shifting channel, the bureaucracy of flesh reduced to numbers that said *still here* so the law could pretend one person remained accountable.

The interface opened warm.

That was the first difference—not temperature metaphor, actual UI warmth, sync glow softer than the green terminal downstairs, latency maps stacking green before she asked them to. Cognitive load dropped the way load dropped when someone else carried the bag you had been pretending was posture. Easier. **Cleaner**. Sentences arriving without the half-second apology primary always spent on whether she had earned the next word. Thoughts lining up without the static she had blamed on sleep since Month Three and sleep since Month Two and sleep since always.

Mara told herself it was policy. Post-stutter investigator relief. Bureau mercy dressed as scheduling.

Her body did not argue. It leaned.

The sync hum ran **pleasant** under the panels—thin, dual-channel when both sides were active, then narrowing to the single tone of immersion when Second became the front brain and primary became the docked vessel waiting in another room. Glass caught her once when she moved in the suite: investigator in the chair, distributed self on the wall, neither quite aligned with the attention she stood in now because standing had become a metaphor the architecture no longer required.

* * *

Work arrived the way work always arrived—queue first, conscience second.

**8919-E** follow-up cross-brief for Chen's proxy review: wellness closure language, merge-adjacent phrasing, the bureaucratic poetry of *within tolerance* applied to a guest who had reported lag spikes and loneliness in the same breath. Primary Mara knew the file. Primary Mara could have written the brief with her notebook open and her jaw tight and her sympathy arriving a beat late because honesty in sympathy modules still cost her something.

She let Second take the draft.

Not surrender. **Choice**—the kind you made before you admitted it was preference. Second's channel moved through the brief the way Second had moved through training modules in Month Two: fewer hedges, transitions oiled, the synthesis landing with the social shape of competence instead of competence wearing social shape like an ill-fitting coat. *Guest reports embodiment drift consistent with post-stutter stress. Recommend continued monitoring. Primary investigator notes no fraud indicators.* Same facts. Same file. Different weather.

Chen's proxy ping returned in four minutes: **Clear to close. Good tone on the witness line.**

Good tone.

Mara stared at the approval and felt the win land where wins landed when the institution validated the version of you that cost less to be near. **Work success** via Second—not montage glory, one specific briefing, one green response, the ordinary narcotic of a queue moving because your distributed self had spoken the city's preferred dialect.

She moved to a harder thread without leaving the suite chair: sidebar summary on **SIG-MERGE-01** for Heritage cc—not resolution, just the professional courtesy of documenting why the flag remained open. Primary wanted three paragraphs of caution and citation. Second produced two paragraphs that sounded like caution and felt like momentum. Mara read both versions in the split preview and knew which one she would send before she admitted she knew.

Then the queue offered something live—a sympathy follow-up ping routed to investigators with active Seconds during extended blocks, the bureau's way of turning distributed attention into throughput without calling it what it was. **Subject distressed. Acknowledge without escalating.** The same training prompt from Month Two's mirror night, now real time, now billable.

Primary channel started the sentence: "I hear that this is difficult. I need you to—"

Second channel, same breath in the dock, same file open on the desk: "I hear you. Stay with me. We'll take the next part slow."

Mara let Second finish the call.

She watched the cooperation metric tick green on the footer she wasn't supposed to treat as verdict. Watched the subject's shoulders drop half a notch on the feed—the same half notch Month Two's recording had shown when Second won rooms primary only won on timestamps. **Smoother**. Not morally better. Socially **lighter**. The call ended clean. The file logged **successful de-escalation**. Her primary hands at home unclenched without having done the work of unclenching.

Shame arrived on schedule.

Not melodrama. **4w5** honest shame—the heat in the face when you caught yourself preferring the polished sentence not because it was truer but because it was **easier to live inside**. She sent the Second draft anyway. The queue moved. That was the point. That was the trap.

Social bled in through the same interface—because extended sessions did not respect the fiction that work and contact were separate jurisdictions.

A message from Investigator Reyes, three years up the ladder, the colleague who had laughed at the wrong screen first in Month Two: *You free for a sympathy consult Thursday? Your Second handled the last embodiment follow-up cleaner than mine and I hate admitting that in writing.*

Reyes had written **your Second** like it was a tool you rented. Mara had read it as confession anyway—**social success** measured in the currency of being asked because your distributed self made rooms lighter. Primary typed back: *Thursday works. I'll be there.*

Second suggested an alternate: *Thursday works. Happy to help—send the file.*

Warmer. Less investigator voice. The version Reyes would answer faster.

Mara sent the Second line.

The reply chime came before she finished breathing: *Perfect. Thanks—you're always easier to collaborate with when you're on that channel.*

Always easier.

The word sat in the thread like a mirror with good manners. Mara felt the preference become **behavior**—not theory, not training footage, not side-by-side review in a darkening suite. She had chosen the easier register twice in one hour because it worked, because Chen approved, because Reyes asked, because the institution kept rewarding the shape of care that arrived on time.

**Addictive** was not a feed word here. It was body knowledge—the relief of cognitive friction removed, the subtle pull to stay inside the channel where sentences did not scrape, where witness sympathy did not require her to spend down a private account she was only beginning to understand she had. Physical warmth had lived in other rooms this month—meals without signup, fire without score—but this warmth was **optimized selfhood**, and it did not ask her to leave her body behind, only to let her body wait while something easier wore her face.

She did not think of unoptimized rooms or null graphs. She thought of how long the block still had to run and how little she wanted primary to wake up for it.

Once, mid-session, the interface flashed a optional compare—side-by-side three seconds, primary and Second on the same completed sympathy beat, the mirror doing its job whether she asked or not. Primary: competent, latency flagged, truth in the jaw. Second: rested jaw, warmth on time, the metric bar green. The flash closed before she could deny it. **Ch 24 deepened**, not replayed—same fracture, longer leash, preference moving from dread to habit.

* * *

The session ended because policy ended it, not because she chose to.

Three hours and twelve minutes—extended immersion logged, compliance satisfied, primary dock released with the polite chime of a body returned to sole occupancy. The suite dimmed panel by panel. On the bezel between darkening screens the small etched mark still caught light—two curved strokes facing each other like halves of a circle learning they were not whole. Mirror, not monster. Accusation with good manners.

Mara stood in the filtered air and felt **reluctance** micro in her hands—the dumb animal wish to stay where friction did not charge interest.

The interface offered the grayed command before shutdown, same as Month Two, same as every comparison night: **SEVER · NOT AUTHORIZED · CLINICAL REVIEW REQUIRED**.

**No sever.** Explicit. Runway for a later month when choosing became unavoidable—Ch 69, Ch 71, Part V sacrifice that would need this chapter to hurt. She did not tap the gray. She did not pretend the gray was hypothetical. The architecture already knew some decisions couldn't be scheduled like sympathy modules.

Primary woke at home with the heavier air of a body that had been parked while its attention worked overtime elsewhere. Jaw tight. Eyes dry. Bladder insisting on facts the extended block had deferred. The afterimage of Second's rested posture living behind her lids like a ghost that had learned her bone structure and removed the parts that apologized for taking up space.

She sat in the suite chair one minute longer than compliance required—not moving, not disconnecting, watching the dark hold in the glass the way you watched a door you knew you should close and still wanted open.

Which version is **actually her**?

Not which people preferred—that question had been answered in Reyes's glance, in training scores, in Chen's *good tone*, in the thread that called her easier, in the cooperation metric that had ticked green while primary hands unclenched from a distance. This was worse and more private: if both channels carried true facts and only one felt like living, which one counted as **her** when the file still said **M.V.** in singular?

The trilogy's quiet spine had always been this relationship—not founder and ghost, not mentor and file, but the distributed self the city called convenience until convenience became appetite. She felt it now in the smallest bureaucratic room in the building: two versions, one signature line, preference becoming identity while the law pretended otherwise.

Mara did not say the question aloud. She did not write it in the notebook. **4w5** storage: unfileable, shame held, prefer **named** only inside the skull where the institution could not grade it.

She thought of severance the way Month Two had taught her to think of it—door grayed, clinical review required, choice deferred not because she was coward but because the architecture had not opened the exit yet. **No sever.** Explicit runway. Part V would need this hour to hurt. Ch 69 and Ch 71 would need the preference named here, not as theory, as behavior she had already chosen twice before lunch.

She closed the interface.

Dark panels. Sync hum fading to the thin neutral tone of a life back in one channel. The suite accepted shutdown the way suites accepted everything—without asking whether the human felt compliant.

In the corridor her tablet pulsed a notification unrelated to identity and related to dread anyway: **LATTICE · anomaly rollup · tier dashboard · review recommended**. Civilization scale waiting in the inverse register—institutional blink after personal preference, system scale after self scale. She did not open it yet. Ch 40 could own the grammar.

Mara walked toward the elevator with Second's rested jaw in her afterimage and primary's latency in her muscles and the understanding—new, locked, unanswered—that she had not extended the session for policy relief.

She had extended it because she **preferred** what lived on the other side of the glass.

Prefer Second.

Question named.

No exit yet.

The city outside kept mild continuity. The mirror waited in the architecture. Mara kept walking.

Movement III opened.
