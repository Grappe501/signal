The fan knew him before the room did.

Month Three, week fourteen. A maintenance pocket above freight cable—not Cord Junction, not the apartment the bureau had climbed to find, but the same grammar: dead relay, manual switch, dictionary under a stripped laptop, paper that did not sync. Mercer had learned to rent fear by the week in buildings the Spine forgot to polish. Six weeks off-grid had not made him heroic. It had made him **thin**—charm spent, public brilliance a costume he no longer owned, **7w8** reduced to maintenance and listening.

He was not running in triumph.

That mattered. The contained headline still called him sufficient—consultant link, solved grammar, open lanes in the footer—and he let it. Letting it was not smirk. Letting it was survival math: a suspect who looked solved was a suspect the field teams stopped prioritizing once the apartment stayed empty and the trail stayed manual. Fear, not guilt. Fear, not confession. The distinction he had carried down four flights in Month Two still held in Month Three, except now it had company.

The hide was smaller than Cord Junction. One chair learned to witness posture. One desk scarred by ring stains from nights stacked on nights. Cables ran along the baseboard because baseboards were where honest people ran cables when they didn't trust walls to stay neutral. Dim light from a clip lamp—no smart bulb, no courtesy fade, no overlay sweetening the window. Through the glass, freight shadows kept the Spine's polish at arm's length. Manual life had always been an aesthetic. Now it was the only tier that didn't sell his shape back to the bureau.

He ate from tins. He slept in shifts. He listened to the public channel on the old radio he had bought secondhand because radios lied slower than feeds and sometimes lying slower was the only way to hear truth arrive without paying for it in metadata. **CONTINUITY REASSURED · CONSULTANT LINK RESOLVED · OPEN LANES.** The city still knew how to sell calm while it moved bodies through procedure. Mercer did not argue with the sidebar. Arguing was performance. Performance was how guilty men behaved in stories.

Something **larger** kept moving.

Not speech. Not briefing. A pulse on the capture rig he had built from salvage—three mismatched feeds stripped to routing bone, no vendor dashboard, no courtesy overlay. The frame glitched once, twice: infrastructure lines converging where merge telemetry had no business meeting continuity trunks, traffic on a schedule that did not consult homicide investigators or deputy directors or consultants who had asked women how often they chose difficulty. The same visual leak he had killed the last screen for in Cord Junction, deeper now, refreshed by channels he did not touch and could not stop watching.

Pattern without memo.

The glitch did not resolve. It **held**—patient, indifferent, not Mercer-shaped—the way the third signature had held left of his activation window when pride still thought left was a mistake you could scroll away from. He watched until the image burned into maintenance memory, the kind you carried in body instead of cloud. He screenshot nothing. Saving would leave teeth.

Mercer leaned back from the desk and felt the animal part of him register scale without needing a diagram. Predates had been Month Two terror—graph left of activation, third signature patient as weather. This was Month Three **maintenance**: the predates had not gone away. They had **multiplied**—threads linking embodiment relays to nodes the public maps never drew, owner windows looking like decoration from a maintenance pocket the same way they had looked like decoration from a consultant nest.

He was frightened.

Not performing it. Not selling witness posture to an empty room. Frightened the way a man was frightened when he understood his charts were late and the architecture did not care who had drawn them last.

* * *

Evidence wall—not a wall, really; a plywood sheet bolted to brick, printouts taped in layers the way cases matched cases when you stopped believing coincidence was a profession. Mercer had carried what mattered out of Cord Junction in the go-bag and rebuilt what he could from back channels and maintenance memory and one soaked page rescued from the sink edge before paper soup took the peaks entirely. The residue page was useless for court and therefore honest—three peaks blurred into ink, illegible now, still rhyming with the band above it when you squinted without hope.

Timeline across the top: activation windows, merge signatures, consultant disclosure dates in bureau font.

**Red** before his start date.

Not one mark. A **band**—early traffic, early peaks, early routing that used the same grammar as his illegal embodiment experiments without requiring his hands. Month Two had been discovery: scroll left until pride died, third signature holding left of the axis, terror replacing ownership. This was **confirmation** in hiding—predates no longer hypothesis, no longer a graph you could unsee if you were smart enough. Predates **infrastructure**—copied architecture, same sinew beneath different skins, the city's body beneath its beauty repeating a blueprint Mercer had thought he was authoring.

He had not authored it.

He had **joined** it late.

The printout showed it without essay: trunk lines branching into embodiment relays with timestamps that predated his name on every axis he had ever defended in symposium rooms. A secondary overlay—pulled from the same back channel he didn't deserve—showed merge telemetry rhyming with traffic that had never passed through his consultant token. Same geometry. Same schedule. Session seals looked like weather from here. Owner windows looked like decoration. **Copied**, or always shared, or always older—the distinction did not comfort. Comfort was not the job.

Witness work was the job.

Mercer traced the red band with a pencil nub—behaviour, not speech—the way he had once traced peaks in rooms where brilliance was performance. The pencil left a faint groove in paper that would not sync. His reflection in the dark tablet glass showed a face without symposium smile: spent, listening, not unmasked so much as **seen** by evidence that did not care about his intentions.

He thought, for one ugly half-second, of explaining it to a room—symposium cadence, consultant charm, the old **7w8** trick of making scale feel like insight. The thought died before it formed a sentence. Explanation was manifesto dressed in better shoes. This room had no audience worth performing for.

Mercer stood in front of the plywood and felt **7w8** collapse toward **1** for one micro-beat—harsh self-judgment, the enneagram snap of a man who had performed brilliance to camouflage private pattern and had run out of performance. Not guilt. Not confession to a crowd. **Fear** primary. He had seen clearly. Seeing clearly was no longer compatible with being origin.

His mouth moved before his mind could build a speech.

Quiet. Not broadcast. Not manifesto. Not villain monologue for feeds that were not listening in a maintenance pocket with dead relays.

"I'm not the first."

The line hung in the fan's whir and did not swell. No score. No triumph. No ownership reclaimed. Just words in an empty room, witness tone, the confession that was not confession because confession implied he had been the author and the axis had already refused that story.

Silence after.

Mercer did not repeat it. Repeating was how men turned fear into performance. He let the line sit where it sat—locked, small, **true**—and looked back at the red band before his start date until his eyes burned with the kind of memory that didn't sync. The fan kept whirring. The clock kept ticking. The kettle on the hot plate stayed cold because cold was what kettles did when men forgot to perform domestic normalcy.

* * *

No manifesto followed.

Behaviour only: he pulled the printout from the tape, folded it wrong, slid it into the go-bag beside cash in inconvenient denominations and the credentials copy that was a file until it wasn't. Paper map in pencil. Spare interface stripped of bloat. Metal keys. Toothbrush still in packaging. Shirt folded wrong. Zip closed. Weight real on his shoulder. He did not pack for flight tonight. He packed for **witness**—evidence portable, trail manual, identity friction on purpose. The same go-bag that had waited half-zipped in Cord Junction now carried Month Three's answer to Month Two's predates: not origin, **node**.

He wiped the capture rig the way he had wiped the data wall—methodically, witness hands, enough for manual life, not enough to perform rage for an empty room. Caches cleared. Local merges saved to nothing. The salvage feeds went dark screen by screen until the desk looked like a man had been living here because a man had been living here—clutter honest, cables slack, nothing staged for search teams.

He did not call Investigator Voss.

Still. The rhyme held—tight jaw, punishment-coffee laugh, raw logs chosen after the UI said which ones mattered. She did not have this axis. He would not hand her a red band before his start date and call it help—not from a maintenance pocket with warrant noise still living in the channels. Scale did not require contact. Contact would shrink what he saw into sidebar grammar.

Mercer crossed to the door and listened—calibration, not paranoia. Old building, old wood. Nothing on the stairs yet. Boots on grit sounded different from boots on policy. The city below kept mild continuity and open lanes and routing icons recovering faith without asking whether faith was a subscription with renewal terms you couldn't read.

Go-bag weight on his shoulder. Real.

Door dark at the frame. City smooth at a distance—the Spine's polish visible through a narrow window, beautiful, employable, wrong in the way wrong had become familiar. He was alone. Warning, not weapon. Suspect becoming **signal** without speech to make it so. The suspect frame from Month Two had not fit. The witness frame did not fit either. Fit was for rooms with lawyers. He had left those rooms on purpose.

He did not look back at the plywood sheet.

The latch clicked when he tested it—manual honesty, twice. Fan still whirring behind him. Paper edges still taped where tape had been. No performance left in the room. No triumph. No capture geometry to perform for. What remained was ordinary mess. What was missing was the man who still believed he had been first.

Night kept moving above freight cable—cables, mild weather, open lanes—while Mercer stood in the dark threshold with fear complete and guilt absent and the line he had said to no audience still sitting in his mouth like a taste that would not sweeten.

He stepped into the service gap where scaffold met cable and the city pretended no one lived who couldn't afford to be seen.

Dark.

Fear, not power.

Witness, not origin.
