# Chapter 1 — Scene 1.1: The Efficient Dream

New Manhattan unrolled below the Spine—tier after tier of morning, each one already on schedule.

Mara stood inside the descending glass with her scuffed CIB mug—real coffee, bitter at the lip where years had worn the enamel thin—and her thumb on the dispatch tablet. She didn't run overlays on transit. **6:47 AM** hit her face raw through the pane: gold on the brow, the old scar above it left alone by any filter. Hers was the only unedited surface in the car.

She trusted mornings. Systems peaked before people performed. The first hour was when records were still embarrassed to lie.

Below her, the city kept its channels open. Vertical parks stacked in clean bands. School pods drifted toward their docking slots; three tiers down, a child flattened her palm to the glass and waved at someone Mara couldn't see—signal passing clean through a barrier that held them apart. Maintenance drones crept the tower seams like polite insects. She remembered when mornings here were loud. Now they arrived pre-mixed, and some part of her still leaned into the sound.

Across the car, a man chewed with mechanical patience while his body sat vacated beside him, eyes aimed at nothing in the car. A couple laughed at separate channels—same sofa, same morning, two punchlines landing a half-second apart. Mara looked away first. Her father had preferred his chair. The one that stayed where you could see it. She did not follow the thought further.

The tablet pulsed.

**Hush Spire 11-A.** A Continuity Steward had flagged **sync stall, 914 seconds.** **Wellness non-response.** Guest registered overnight. Classification: **elevated residence anomaly.**

Not homicide. Not yet.

The stall duration was wrong for Platinum tier. She checked the timestamps again the way other people checked locks—**trigger 0641**, dispatch **0647**. Six minutes without a response. Someone sat on this before CIB got the call.

Her earpiece clicked live.

"Anomaly first," Deputy Director Marisa Chen said, dry as always, already reading the same file. "Label later. Don't touch the Second until you know whose it is."

Mara trusted that order. Chen trusted her to look before she named.

The car chimed at the Hush District stop—harmonic, flawless, the sound designed to mean *welcome*—and the transit hum fell away behind her. Wealth had its own acoustics here: padded, filtered, forgiving of nothing human. Mara stepped onto muted flooring and heard her own heel strike like an error in the mix. CIB housing had never looked like this. Beauty without warmth.

In the lobby, a building liaison Second held the posture of perfect courtesy, face arranged for empty welcome, eyes meeting hers without the small stumbles of a human who had just woken. Correct. Empty. She did not trust Seconds that performed welcome on schedule. Mara nodded and kept moving, glass walls on every side, the city beyond corrected and still.

The penthouse elevator took whatever remained of the city hum and deleted it—not lowered, deleted. Buildings were not supposed to sound this dead. Mara stood in the cab and listened to her pulse fill the space like an insult. The indicator climbed. **11-A.** A beat of nothing. Then the doors opened on light too even to be morning, as if the sun, too, had been corrected overnight.

The biometric seal accepted her CIB override without hesitation.
