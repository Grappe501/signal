# DELIVERABLE 2 · Market Position Report

## Positioning statement

**For readers of** literary near-future SF and prestige dystopia (*Severance*, *Klara and the Sun*, *Black Mirror* with a novel's patience)—  
**The Second Self** is a speculative thriller  
**that** uses an impossible murder investigation to expose how optimization, digital doubles, and institutional calm are already consuming the people who built them—  
**unlike** tech-thriller minimalism, it earns its length with family-scale horror and civilization-scale set pieces.

## Price band / format

| Format | Fit |
|--------|-----|
| Hardcover literary SF | **Primary** |
| Trade paperback | **Strong** |
| Ebook | **Strong** — binge corridors 28–31, 47–53 |
| Audio | **High potential** — multi-POV, institutional voices, set pieces |

## Discoverability gaps (packaging, not prose)

1. Title search collision — needs series string on cover/Amazon  
2. No subtitle hook for thriller readers ("A Novel" is insufficient)  
3. Comp titles must lead marketing — readers won't self-identify from "Second Self" alone

## Competitive moat

- **Procedural + family + god-scale** in one volume  
- **Empty Chair** and **cold aisle** — shareable domestic/industrial horror  
- **Trilogy ignition** without Book One feeling incomplete

# DELIVERABLE 6 · Commercial Risk Report

1. **Length (~131k)** — fatigued before Ch 28 Veil lock (~40% mark for slow readers)  
2. **Investigation valley (Ch 20–24)** — procedure rhythm; bureau nouns; velocity dip after Part I high  
3. **Terminology stack (Ch 12–15)** — case IDs before emotional payoff fully lands  
4. **Archive breath (Ch 22 · 35)** — catalog voice; less body; skimmers bail

## Top five reasons readers recommend

## Section 11 · Elevator test

### 15 seconds

"It's *Severance* as a literary thriller: a detective investigates a murder in a world where everyone has a legal robot double—and discovers the system is already hiding crimes too big for one body to hold."

### 60 seconds

"Near-future New York runs on Continuity—AI-bodied Seconds, beauty filters, civic scores. Investigator Mara Voss is called to a Platinum murder where the victim's digital duplicate sits frozen in the chair while the record says everything's fine. The case opens into impossible data: one consciousness in three places, predictions that retract, a Mars founder who isn't dead but isn't right, and thousands of bodies stored as 'idle.' It's a detective story that becomes a horror story about optimization—the comfort layer eating the people who built it. Ends with the architecture going public. Book one of three."

### 3 minutes

Add: Prologue Adrian Vale on Mars with a tremor the world smooths away. Mara's father echo in empty chairs. Noah and Iona at breakfast—Running Double as normal life. Dubai artist sees cracks under perfect cities. Mercer not villain but witness to infrastructure that predates him. The Veil drops mid-book—city goes raw. Vale in a hold room asks what silence sounded like. Mara walks a warehouse aisle of preserved Seconds. Mirror chamber: she meets her own rested duplicate. Epilogue: anonymous upload—*Connection established.* Tone is literary, institutional, grief-forward—not pulp. Comp: *Severance*, Ishiguro, *Black Mirror* with a novel's patience. Built for TV. Franchise question is identity under optimization, not killer robots.
