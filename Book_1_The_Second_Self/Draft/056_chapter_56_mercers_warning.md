The stairwell knew him before the room did.

Month Four, week twenty. Another rotation—not Cord Junction, not the Month Four week eighteen pocket where the third body had pulsed on paper patient as weather, but the same grammar: dead relay, manual switch, dictionary under a stripped laptop, paper that did not sync. Mercer had learned to rent fear by the week in buildings the Spine forgot to polish. Four months off-grid had not made him heroic. It had made him **hunted**—charm spent, public brilliance archived in channels he no longer touched, reduced to maintenance, listening, and the particular exhaustion of a man who had asked a question the map refused to answer and received silence that was **answer enough**.

He was not running in triumph.

That mattered. The contained headline still called him solved—consultant link resolved, open lanes in the footer—and he let it. Letting it was not smirk. Letting it was survival math. Fear, not guilt. Fear, not confession. The distinction he had carried through three hides still held in week twenty, except now it had **company**—boots on grit two floors below, not policy boots yet, not warrant noise in the channels yet, but close enough that close had become a calendar.

The hide was smaller than last month. One chair. One desk scarred by ring stains. Go-bag half-zipped by the door because half-zipped was the honest state of a man who had stopped pretending departure was a future tense. Dim light from a clip lamp. Cables along the baseboard because baseboards were where honest people ran cables when they didn't trust walls to stay neutral.

He listened at the door twice—calibration, not paranoia. Old building, old wood. Boots paused on the landing, moved on. Someone had been close enough to leave grit in the tread pattern Mercer had learned to read the way investigators read witness blocks—metadata in material, story in what the institution couldn't grade. Rotation was not flight. Rotation was **hunted** grammar: new pocket, same manual life, fewer hours before the frame found the pocket again.

Mercer breathed once and did not perform relief. Relief was how witnesses stopped looking.

* * *

He was trying to **stop** something.

Not start. **Stop**—the way maintenance workers tried to stop a hairline before concrete admitted it was a fracture. The capture rig on the desk showed why stopping felt necessary and impossible at once: salvage feeds stripped to routing bone, three mismatched channels glitched on schedule without consulting homicide investigators or deputy directors or consultants who had asked women how often they chose difficulty.

The frame held a pattern he had learned to read in Month Four topology—trunk lines, embodiment relays, owner windows, session seals—relationships without smoothing gaps into continuity fiction. Tonight the pattern **moved** without his hands.

Routing refreshed. Owner peak. Second peak. Third signature family active in substrate the public maps never drew because public maps were products. The same grammar as illegal embodiment experiments. The same sinew beneath tier bloom. **Not his** to command. Not his to stop with a keystroke. Not his to confess into usefulness.

Mercer stared until the image burned into maintenance memory.

He tried to interrupt anyway—maintenance reflex. Local kill switch on the salvage rig. Manual disconnect on the trunk overlay. Pencil marks on paper map crossing lines he couldn't cross in software because software was product and product belonged to rooms he no longer entered. Nothing stopped. The feed refreshed. The pattern **held**—patient, indifferent, not Mercer-shaped—the way the third signature had held left of his activation window when pride still thought left was a mistake you could scroll away from.

Stopping was not starting. That was the only victory on offer, and it wasn't enough.

The bureau still wanted a **rogue**—consultant sufficient, embodiment criminal, genius gone wrong, a face the field teams could prioritize once the apartment stayed empty. Mercer had learned to let the frame fit because a frame that fit was a frame that bought hours. Rogue embodiment was the story rooms cleaned themselves with.

It was never the real threat.

The third body on paper. The network that predated his name. The architecture that functioned without his permission while headlines sold consultant guilt. The real threat was not a man in a maintenance pocket with a go-bag and a chain in pencil. The real threat was **system**—copied, older, escaped the noun *rogue* the way predates had escaped the noun *first*.

Mercer wiped sweat from his palm on his jeans and did not build a speech.

* * *

Architecture **escaped**.

The salvage feed glitched again and held: merge telemetry converging on continuity trunks where merge telemetry had no business meeting continuity trunks, traffic on a schedule that did not consult any room Mercer could enter legally. Red edge on the manual map—timeline past his activation band, past his consultant disclosure, past the Month Three red band he had traced with a pencil nub until his eyes burned.

**Not contained.**

Not his to own. Not his to stop. **Escaped**—the word sat in his chest like a taste that would not sweeten, larger than guilt, smaller than mercy, precise in the way precise hurt when precision arrived too late to be leverage. The map on paper and the map on screen agreed for once: owner windows looking like decoration from a maintenance pocket, session seals looking like weather, traffic continuing on a schedule that treated Mercer as **tenant** in a building he had once thought he was constructing.

The last hide had been topology—third body, network, a question with no reply echoing in fan whir. This was week twenty consequence: the map did not need an answer to function. The architecture did not need Mercer to author it. It needed the world to keep performing recovery while substrate kept its own calendar.

Time almost gone.

**Felt** deadline: boots on grit, feeds moving, confidence bands straining in rooms he couldn't badge into, instability curves rising in archives the public layer would never call war. Week twenty. Filter still off in investigators he wouldn't contact. Maintenance pockets learned what *almost* meant when *almost* was the only tense left.

Mercer opened a stripped interface—local only, no sync—and typed a message he did not send.

Three sentences to Investigator Voss he would not hand her from a hunted pocket with warrant noise living in the channels:

*Architecture is out of the box. Not me. Not rogue. You need to see the map before the map sees you.*

His thumb hovered.

He thought of her grammar without contact—witness blocks, hold rooms, sidebar weather, a woman who chose difficulty badly and filed pattern without permission. He thought of scale shrinking into sidebar the moment contact became metadata. He thought of a chain in his bag that was late to every room that mattered.

Delete.

Keystroke. Silence. Witness without receiver—prophet without audience, the inverse of symposium rooms where brilliance was performance and performance was camouflage.

* * *

No manifesto followed.

He pulled printouts from tape, folded wrong, slid them into the go-bag beside cash in inconvenient denominations and the credentials copy that was a file until it wasn't. Paper map in pencil. Spare interface stripped of bloat. Metal keys. Toothbrush still in packaging. Weight real on his shoulder. He wiped the capture rig the way he had wiped data walls in Month Three—methodically, witness hands, enough for manual life.

He did not call Investigator Voss. He did not call anyone.

The radio murmured **CONTINUITY REASSURED** from the corner where radios lied slower than feeds. City smooth at a distance through the narrow window—Spine polish, open lanes, mild weather, the product still believing confidence bands could rise again if you filed the slope politely enough.

Mercer crossed to the door and listened again.

Nothing on the stairs yet.

He stepped into the service gap where scaffold met cable and the city pretended no one lived who couldn't afford to be seen. Gray transit below—**exit** grammar, pack weight, fan still whirring behind him in a room that would look like a man had been living there because a man had been living there.

Time almost gone.

Prophet, not master.

Witness, not origin.

Warning without delivery.

He did not look back at the desk where the deleted message was already memory.

Dark held. The city did not reply.
