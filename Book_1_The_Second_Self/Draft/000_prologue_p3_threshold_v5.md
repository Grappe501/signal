The exterior door opened.

Red light flooded the visor—not corrected, not softened, not passed through the polite filters of the life he'd built on Earth. Raw Martian afternoon. A colour that wasn't quite red: iron, rust, dried blood in powder form, lit by a sun smaller than the one he'd been born under in Pretoria and learned to ignore because the maths of survival didn't care about nostalgia.

Inside the module: human order. Outside: a world that had never made room for human noise. The threshold was a line in the dust. He crossed it.

Sound deleted itself.

Not lowered. Deleted. Commentary in his ear cut mid-word—an analyst saying **"histor—"** and then nothing. Suite hum gone. The distant pulse of everyone watching—gone. His world narrowed to fan hiss, link tone, breath counting in the helmet. Wealth had deleted sound in penthouse elevators on Earth; Mars deleted it properly.

His boot touched regolith.

Dust behaved like dust: slow, indifferent, slightly ashamed to be disturbed. The print stayed—clean-edged depression in rust-coloured fines. First human punctuation on a page that had never needed language.

He stood in it long enough for every camera to understand this wasn't a hop, not a flag, not a logo placement for a board meeting. EVA timer on the HUD started anyway—**00:00:47**, **00:00:48**, **00:00:49**—and he let it run. Proof wasn't a pose at the airlock. Proof was weight over time.

He looked back at Farshore.

White module. Landing struts. Bundled cable runs like veins. Human-small against a horizon that didn't care. Phobos a pinprick if you knew where to look—and he did, because he'd been looking at sky maps since he was a kid in a bedroom full of dead cities while other children collected football cards or whatever normal children collected. He'd wanted extinction museums. He'd gotten one. He was standing in it.

Wrong kind of alive.

He walked because stunts were for people who still had margins.

He lifted his boot. Set it down one metre out. Then another. The walk had started whether the world was ready or not.
