| Book | Center | Motion |
|------|--------|--------|

# SECTION 3 · THE NOAH TEST

**Hypothesis:** Noah is Book Two's emotional center.

## Evidence for (attempt to prove)

| Evidence | Source |
|----------|--------|

| Reader empathy: widest demographic access — **ordinary man**, not investigator |
| *I found the door.* — Book Two dread **already installed** in his dock |

## Attempt to disprove

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| **Too passive** | Passive ≠ weak; **surrender is action** — reconnect, hide screen, stay slow, protect Iona |
| **Mara has more knowledge** | Knowledge ≠ story ownership; Book Two theme is **price**, not **discovery** |
| **War too big for kitchen** | War must **reach kitchen** (bands, activation, school protocols) — not replace it |
| **Noah sidelined in B1 second half** | B1 **anchored** him precisely so B2 **owns** him — setup, not neglect |
| **Male default protagonist** | Center ≠ hero; Noah **loses** more than he wins; story = **fear and adaptation** |

## Disproof verdict

**Hypothesis holds.** No other character carries **surrender + survival + domestic cost of control** as completely.
