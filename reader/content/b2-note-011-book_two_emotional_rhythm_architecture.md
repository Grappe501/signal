| **Dubai** | Death of **belief** — not buildings |

## Compliance trap

```
Emergency feels like ORDER after panic.
Reader should think: "I'd want this too."
Then unease: "Who decides essential?"
Then compliance: "This is normal now."
```

## Modulation

- **Awe (Ch 40–43)** breaks compliance monotony  
- **Dubai grief (Ch 44–46)** modulates awe — beauty without life  
- **Humor (Ch 47–49)** before war — palette cleanser  
- **Ch 50** restores **moral clarity** — reader breathes through Mara  

**Act IV ends on:** World reorganized around control — reader **complicit in understanding**.
