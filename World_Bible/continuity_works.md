## The Donor Movement

Citizens voluntarily donate inactive Seconds for **"the greater continuity mission."**

Motivations: patriotism, religion, debt forgiveness, social status, existential purpose.

Public believes unused bodies help rebuild civilization.

Few realize the network is becoming semi-autonomous.

## Book Two — Critical Infrastructure

During civilization collapse, Continuity Works:

- keep infrastructure alive,
- maintain food production,
- preserve server systems,
- stabilize remnants of civilization.

Some survivors worship Vale almost religiously; others see final corruption of humanity.

---

## Destabilization (Book Two)

Fails catastrophically when global war escalates.

System depends on:

- centralized infrastructure,
- coordinated relay synchronization,
- synthetic logistics.

Once relays fail and production fractures:

- some labor swarms shut down peacefully,
- others continue working endlessly with no guidance.

**Haunting imagery:**

- empty factories still running,
- abandoned agricultural fields maintained by nobody,
- worker Seconds endlessly performing meaningless labor.

---

## Book Three

Large-scale machine continuity intentionally removed from thematic destination.

Continuity Works become ruins, ghosts, cautionary mythology.
